About Me

Hi! I'm Isabela Camacho, a junior Physics major at Santa Clara University with minors in Mathematics and Computer Science & Engineering. My research experiences have spanned from biophysics, to computational neuroscience, to machine learning.

In summer 2025, I worked at Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory in David Klindt's Lab, studying when linear probes fail and how sparse autoencoders and dictionary learning can reveal more interpretable latent structure. Previously, I interned in Dr. David J. Anderson’s lab at Caltech (WAVE ’24), developing mechanistic models of neuropeptide release to explain line-attractor dynamics in VMHvl. I’m now a research assistant in Prof. Michelle McCulley’s lab at SCU, using molecular dynamics to analyze protein–DNA chimeras and connect simulation results to protein folding and stability.

Earlier, under Dr. Shyam Gajavelli at the University of Florida, I used computational sequence analysis to study Shannon entropy in green fluorescent protein (GFP) to predict conserved residues. Looking ahead, I plan to pursue graduate study in computational neuroscience or biophysics—with the long-term goal of turning principled models into tools that can impact human health.
