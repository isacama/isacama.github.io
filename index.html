About Me

Hi! I'm Isabela Camacho, a junior Physics major at Santa Clara University with minors in Mathematics and Computer Science & Engineering. I build computational models of biological systems and validate them with real data—bridging theory and experiment.

In summer 2025, I worked at Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory (Klindt Lab), studying when linear probes fail and how sparse autoencoders and dictionary learning can reveal more interpretable latent structure. Previously, I interned in Dr. David J. Anderson’s lab at Caltech (SFP ’24), developing mechanistic models of neuropeptide release to explain line-attractor dynamics in VMHvl. I’m now a research assistant in Prof. Michelle McCulley’s lab at SCU, using molecular dynamics to analyze protein–DNA chimeras and connect simulation results to protein folding and stability.

Earlier, under Dr. Shyam Gajavelli, I used computational sequence analysis to study Shannon entropy in green fluorescent protein (GFP) to predict conserved residues. Looking ahead, I plan to pursue graduate study in computational neuroscience or biophysics—with the long-term goal of turning principled models into tools that can impact human health.
